Method and curiosity, from Sardinia to the world.

Management engineer working in supply chain at EssilorLuxottica, shaped by Emilia-Romagna, Japan, and a lifelong passion for how things work.

See my projects Get in touch

I was born in Sardinia, and growing up between the sea and the mountains left me with a mindset I still carry in how I work: the ability to adapt to very different contexts without losing a clear sense of direction.

I attended a language-focused high school, studying English, French and German alongside Italian and Sardinian — a foundation that still shapes how comfortable I am moving between cultures.

I studied Management Engineering in Reggio Emilia, bachelor's and master's degree. During my master's, I spent a semester at Nagoya University in Japan, observing up close the organizational methods that make Japanese industrial production a global benchmark.

Outside of work, my passion is mechanics in every form: drones, planes, helicopters, boats. At 16, I built my first drone from scratch. I also love programming, and it's something I'd like to keep learning every day.
